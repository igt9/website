Fair Water Distribution Management Plan (DMA Focus)

Internship project at KUKL (Maharajgunj Branch) focused on assessing and simulating the existing distribution pattern in DMA 10.1 and preparing a fair water distribution plan based on population and demand, supported by GIS mapping and NRW analysis workflows.

Contact Jump to Overview

Overview

This project documents my internship work under the initiative “Collaborative Research and Capacity Strengthening for Enhancing Water Security in Kathmandu Valley (Phase-1)” and my assigned theme: “Fair Water Distribution Management Plan in one DMA”. The internship ran for three months (mid-May to mid-August 2025) and my core objective was to assess and simulate the existing distribution pattern in DMA 10.1 and prepare a fair distribution plan based on population and demand.

Internship Context

  • Placement: KUKL, Maharajgunj Branch
  • Theme: Fair Water Distribution Management Plan in one DMA
  • Supervisor: Er. Narayan Karna
  • Core DMA: DMA 10.1 (fair distribution assessment + simulation)

Workstreams

My internship activities covered three main workstreams: (1) GIS-based pipe network mapping, (2) NRW calculation, and (3) fair water distribution assessment & planning in DMA 10.1.

1) Pipe Network Mapping (QGIS)

A short training was conducted to equip interns with skills for laying out water supply pipes and valves in QGIS. After that, mapping tasks were carried out in coordination with valve operators.

  • Mapped areas: Tarakeshwor & Tokha Municipality
  • Coordination: Valve operators (field coordination) and site offices
  • Output: Updated pipe network layer and pipe database summaries (material/diameter)

2) NRW Calculation (DMA 7.1)

NRW calculation is a key first step in NRW management — it involves collecting monthly readings at customer connections and estimating supplied water that did not generate revenue due to issues like unauthorized consumption or metering defects.

  • DMA: DMA 7.1
  • Approach: Monthly meter reading-based calculation and comparison across scenarios
  • Main difficulty: Inconsistent meter reading data and unreliable remarks

3) Fair Water Distribution Plan (DMA 10.1)

KUKL’s fair water distribution planning aims to allocate water more equitably so supply matches demand as closely as possible. My role was to assess and analyze how the fair water supply plan performs in DMA 10.1 and whether it supports equitable access.

Key Findings

Comparing meter readings with model-predicted outflows showed major gaps across most locations. Actual supply was about 35–55% lower than expected in areas such as Chamati 3, Balaju, and New Colony, while a few sites like Janahit Tole and Lekhnath Sadak aligned more closely.

These discrepancies suggest significant NRW influences (leakage, meter inaccuracies, unauthorized consumption). From a fairness perspective, even if the model allocates water “equally,” the real supply can be much lower in some communities — undermining equity and transparency.

Challenges

  • Discrepancy in model outputs vs. meter-based reality
  • Unreliable remarks/address in meter reading data
  • Significant unmetered customers, affecting analysis and equity assessment

Recommendations / Ways Forward

Based on the observed gaps between modeled and actual supply, the fair distribution plan should be supported by:

  • Leakage control and targeted investigations in low-supply zones
  • Metering improvements (reduce unmetered connections, improve meter reliability)
  • Model recalibration using updated observations and improved field data quality
  • Better data remarks/address standardization for future monitoring and fairness checks
